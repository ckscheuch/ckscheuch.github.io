Price Efficiency in Markets with Stochastic Latency

Nikolaus Hautsch, Christoph Scheuch, Stefan Voigt

Abstract

The distributed ledger technology, better known as blockchain, promises fast settlements of transactions on secure ledgers at very low costs. However, while ensuring systtem security, distributed systems introduce stochastic verification time. We argue that this stochastic latency imposes constraints on price efficiency as it exposes arbitrageurs to price risk. We derive a measure for the contribution of stochastic latency to relative price efficiency and show that it accounts for 37% of observed price differences in Bitcoin markets.
